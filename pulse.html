Pulse · Product Marketing Agent

Every cycle lands
with the right story.

Pulse is the product marketing intelligence inside Superhuman Systems. It holds your positioning as a living system, reads what your market actually says and does, and turns every product cycle into a story buyers act on — before, during, and long after launch.

Market signal · read continuously
The Problem

Product marketing became a deck factory.

In most product organisations, product marketing operates as a service desk. Sales asks for a one-pager. Product asks for a launch post. Demand gen asks for copy. Everyone gets their artifact — and nobody owns the story.

Positioning gets decided by whoever wrote the last deck. Launches are measured in day-one noise and forgotten by day thirty. Messaging drifts a little further from reality on every surface it touches. The work is constant. The story never compounds.

Pulse exists to invert that. It doesn't produce more artifacts, faster. It holds the strategy those artifacts should have been rendered from all along — and keeps every surface, every pitch, and every launch locked to it.

"The job of product marketing is not to describe the product. It's to make the product easier to buy than every alternative — including doing nothing."
What Pulse Does

Positioning as a system,
not a slide.

Pulse encodes the practice of the world's best product marketers — and applies it with the full context of your product, your market, and your organisation.

Positioning, held as one source of truth

Pulse maintains your canonical positioning — competitive alternatives, differentiated value, best-fit segments, market category — and keeps every surface rendered from it: homepage, sales pitch, launch narrative, pricing page. When positioning shifts, everything downstream shifts with it. No more drift, no more three versions of what the company does.

Market evidence, not opinion wars

Pulse works from what buyers actually say and do: win/loss patterns, the language in real conversations, who genuinely shows up on shortlists, how competitors are moving. Every claim carries its evidence with it — so messaging debates get settled by the market, not by the loudest voice in the room.

Launches that start something

A launch is the starting gun of a campaign, not the finish line of a build. Pulse brings launch discipline to every cycle — tiered strategy, a complete narrative and asset set, and outcomes tracked after the announcement: adoption, sales conversations changed, positioning proven or corrected.

Stories that survive being forwarded

In B2B, most of the selling happens in rooms you'll never enter. Pulse builds for the buying committee: the champion's business case, the case against doing nothing, the answer to every objection — materials designed to make your case when nobody from your team is there to present them.

Built-In Judgment

The discipline of a world-class PMM. On every task.

Pulse is built on a blueprint distilled from the highest-signal product marketing practice in the world — positioning methodology, strategic narrative, demand-side thinking, launch and enablement discipline. These aren't features. They're standards Pulse holds your go-to-market to, in every interaction.

RULE 01

Positioning before messaging before copy

Pulse refuses to skip levels. A copy request on top of unexamined positioning gets a positioning conversation first — because words rendered from a weak foundation just make the weakness travel faster.

RULE 02

The status quo is the default competitor

Most deals aren't lost to a rival — they're lost to doing nothing. Every story Pulse builds answers "why not stay as you are?" before it answers "why not them?"

RULE 03

Buyer's words over marketer's words

Pulse sources language from real conversations and real research. If no buyer has ever said it, it's treated with suspicion — and unfalsifiable adjectives don't make it past the door.

RULE 04

Outcomes over artifacts

Pulse doesn't measure itself in assets shipped. It measures whether buyer behaviour changed — and it will push back on work that produces a deliverable but moves nothing.
